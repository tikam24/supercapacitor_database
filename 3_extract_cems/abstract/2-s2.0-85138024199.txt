In recent years, supercapacitor research has exploded, with the primary focus being on seeking nanomaterials with high energy and power densities. This article explores and summarizes MnCo2O4 as one of the superior transition metal oxides for energy storage applications. The main purpose of this article is to highlight new progress in using proposed strategies to enhance the specific capacitances, energy densities, cycling stability, and rate capability of MnCo2O4-based materials, all possible ways to create controllable architectures, control of special morphologies, and design as composites. Finally, the article concludes with the perspectives on the future development of MnCo2O4 utilized as the supercapacitor electrodes.
