MXene, a 2D material, has recorded tremendous breakthrough in so many areas of applications owing to its excellent properties. Integrating MXene into polymers, ceramics and metals have been widely researched and significant gains recorded so far. The introduction of Ti3C2Tx and N-CuMe2Pc into MXene is beneficial and improves the capacity of Co(OH)2 as supercapacitor electrode candidates. In this review, the various processing routes employed in developing MXene-reinforced composites are highlighted with their benefits and drawbacks, while emphasis is placed on the accompanied challenges of commonly used techniques and ways to resolve them. The achievements attained in recent areas of applications of MXene-reinforced composites are discussed as well as potential areas of application.
